### Slide 2 — 📌 Introduction
- Electric vehicles are a key part of sustainable transportation
- Conventional charging depends on physical cables and connectors
- Wireless charging offers a contactless alternative using electromagnetic induction

---

### Slide 3 — ❗ Problem Statement
- Charging cables are prone to physical wear and weather exposure
- Manual plugging/unplugging reduces convenience
- Exposed connectors raise safety concerns
- Cable-based systems limit automation potential

---

### Slide 4 — 🎯 Objectives
- Design a transmitter coil circuit to generate a magnetic field
- Design a receiver coil circuit to capture induced voltage
- Implement a rectifier circuit for AC-to-DC conversion
- Demonstrate functional wireless power transfer
- Evaluate performance across varying coil distances

---

### Slide 5 — 🏗️ System Architecture
- Transmitter Side: Power Source → Driver Circuit → Transmitter Coil
- Receiver Side: Receiver Coil → Rectifier Circuit → DC Output → Battery
- No physical electrical connection between transmitter and receiver sides

### Slide 6 — ⚙️ Working Principle
1. Driver circuit excites transmitter coil with alternating current
2. Transmitter coil generates a time-varying magnetic field
3. Receiver coil captures the field and induces an AC voltage
4. Rectifier converts AC voltage into DC
5. DC output is used to charge the vehicle battery
